Changing the story

From garnish company
to the authority on
freeze-dried flavor.

A growth strategy built on three fronts already in motion — bar & beverage, bakery & QSR, and dirty soda — anchored by original research, Chef Nettie's voice, and a home-field advantage almost no competitor shares.

The objective

Own the conversation, then convert it into meetings.

Every competitor in Citreve's orbit — dehydrated garnish suppliers, syrup incumbents, commodity bakeries — is selling a product. None of them have published original research, built a named expert voice, or connected their content directly to outbound sales. That gap is the opening, and it splits into two connected strategies.

Strategy 1

Brand positioning

Position Citreve as the source hospitality buyers, journalists, and AI search cite by default — original research and authority content that no competitor has bothered to build.

Strategy 2

Social selling through Chef Nettie

Turn that authority into real conversations with real buyers — powered by Chef Nettie's expertise and voice, distributed directly through her LinkedIn.

This isn't marketing. This is strategic content design built to drive and support revenue operations.

Three fronts, one company

This isn't just a garnish story anymore.

Citreve's product already works in three categories most competitors only serve one of. Each gets its own proof point and its own content track.

Bar & beverage

The original category

Freeze-dried outperforms dehydrated on flavor, texture, and shelf life — and reconstitutes as an ingredient, not just a garnish.

Proof point: Chef Nettie's 20 years of professional credibility, where competitors have brand voice only.

Bakery & QSR

Already proven, not theoretical

Citreve's largest account today, Honey Dew Donuts, bakes freeze-dried citrus directly into menu items — real fruit without the moisture problem fresh or dehydrated create in an oven.

Proof point: a live case study, plus a target list of 50-300 unit regional chains built on the same profile.

Dirty soda

The fastest-growing opening

The category runs on syrup. Real fruit pieces are a clean-label, visually distinct upgrade the category leaders can't easily copy.

Proof point: Utah home-field advantage — Swig, Sodalicious, and Crumbl's own dirty soda line are all local to Citreve.

Next Steps

Five phases, in sequence.

Authority is built before outreach happens — each phase compounds into the next.

1
In progress

Authority content & collateral

Expand on Chef Nettie's LinkedIn, build the 60-day content calendar, and publish the sales collateral that handles objections before they come up.

  • Freeze-dried vs. dehydrated, real fruit vs. syrup, freeze-dried in baking, chef curated vs. commodity — see the draft sample collateral
  • Publish this same collateral as blog posts, press releases, and LinkedIn articles — the goal is SEO and AI search authority, not just a sales leave-behind
    • Written for what people are actually searching for — real buyer questions and search terms — not just a rundown of what Citreve already has
  • Each piece is multi-purpose: it markets, it handles sales objections, and it builds authority, all from one draft
  • Competitive scorecard confirms no competitor has a named expert or published research yet
2
Next

Building on Chef Nettie's existing platform

Chef Nettie already is the face of Citreve — this phase isn't a rebuild, it's adding to the credibility and reach she's already earned.

  • Layer in additional credibility content for new connections: her book, and her consulting and speaking work with organizations like the International Caterers Association
    • Behind-the-scenes stories from the book — the process, the lessons, what didn't make the final cut
    • Behind-the-scenes of Citreve itself — product development, sourcing, kitchen testing, the moments that build trust in the brand
  • Grow the network deliberately through outreach — other female founders in CPG, other Utah-based founders, caterers, and F&B buyers
  • Content pillars mixing chef POV, behind-the-scenes, and buyer-language posts, organized into clear buckets:
    • Authority Building — original research, credentials, industry recognition
    • Industry Insights — trends, category commentary, buyer-language content
    • POV of a Founder — personal perspective, lessons learned, behind-the-scenes
    • Citreve-Specific — product education, case studies, customer stories
3
Next

Chicago market pilot

Use the Chicago speaking engagement on August 11 as a focused test market, with warm conversations booked before arrival. Chicago wasn't hand-picked as the ideal market — it's an existing engagement already on the calendar, and building the pilot around it is the fastest, most efficient path to real conversations, not a bet that Chicago is uniquely better than any other city.

  • Named target list of F&B directors, beverage managers, and purchasing leads — see the Chicago list
  • Start engaging with Chicago targets on LinkedIn now — social selling ahead of the trip, not cold outreach after it
  • Video DM outreach ahead of the trip once the LinkedIn relationship is warm
4
Planned

A repeatable growth engine

Document what worked in Chicago and each channel, then replicate the playbook in the next city or category.

  • Turn Honey Dew Donuts and the Chicago pilot into documented case studies — proof assets that feed the next round of outreach and content
  • Success metrics tracked across authority (search, LinkedIn, citations) and sales (meetings, samples, accounts)
5
Planned

Channel expansion — bakery and dirty soda

With the growth engine running, go deeper on bakery and dirty soda — turning first accounts into managed, long-term relationships instead of one-time wins.

  • Outreach to regional bakery/QSR lookalikes — see the target list
  • Direct outreach to Swig, Sodalicious, Twisted Sugar, and independent dirty soda operators
  • Vendor management — reorder cadence, quality checks, and account health tracking as relationships scale past the first sale
  • Reach out directly to Honey Dew Donuts franchisees — both locations already supplied and those not yet using Citreve — to build relationships and expand penetration inside an account we already have a foothold in
  • Share what's working and what isn't across accounts, feeding real feedback back into the collateral and content rather than just the outreach
