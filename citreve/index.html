The objective

Own the conversation, then convert it into meetings.

Every competitor in Citreve's orbit — dehydrated garnish suppliers, syrup incumbents, commodity bakeries — is selling a product. None of them have published original research, built a named expert voice, or connected their content directly to outbound sales. That gap is the opening: position Citreve as the source hospitality buyers, journalists, and AI search cite by default, then use that authority to book real conversations with real buyers.

Three fronts, one company

This isn't just a garnish story anymore.

Citreve's product already works in three categories most competitors only serve one of. Each gets its own proof point and its own content track.

Bar & beverage

The original category

Freeze-dried outperforms dehydrated on flavor, texture, and shelf life — and reconstitutes as an ingredient, not just a garnish.

Proof point: Chef Nettie's 20 years of professional credibility, where competitors have brand voice only.

Bakery & QSR

Already proven, not theoretical

Citreve's largest account today, Honey Dew Donuts, bakes freeze-dried citrus directly into menu items — real fruit without the moisture problem fresh or dehydrated create in an oven.

Proof point: a live case study, plus a target list of 50-300 unit regional chains built on the same profile.

Dirty soda

The fastest-growing opening

The category runs on syrup. Real fruit pieces are a clean-label, visually distinct upgrade the category leaders can't easily copy.

Proof point: Utah home-field advantage — Swig, Sodalicious, and Crumbl's own dirty soda line are all local to Citreve.
