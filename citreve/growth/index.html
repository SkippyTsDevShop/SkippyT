05 / Growth & repetition

Documenting what works, then doing it again.

The first 90 days generate proof. This is what happens with that proof — how it's measured, turned into case studies, managed as real accounts, and replicated in the next city or channel.

Success metrics

Two scoreboards, tracked together.

Authority and sales are different timelines — authority compounds slowly, sales conversations happen in weeks. Both get tracked so neither gets ignored.

Authority metrics

Is the content working?

  • Organic search visibility for target keywords
  • AI search citations — is Citreve showing up in ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude answers
  • LinkedIn audience growth and engagement rate
  • Backlinks and mentions from hospitality/industry publications
Sales metrics

Is it converting to revenue?

  • Qualified meetings booked (Chicago and beyond)
  • Response rate to social selling outreach
  • Samples requested and new wholesale accounts opened
  • Revenue influenced by content and outreach, tracked against average sale size
Case study pipeline

Two proof assets, in progress.

Bakery/QSR proof

Honey Dew Donuts

Citreve's largest current account, already using freeze-dried citrus in menu items. Documented as a before/after case study, it becomes the leave-behind for every regional bakery/QSR lookalike on the target list.

Market pilot proof

Chicago pilot

Whatever converts from the Chicago list — hotel F&B, catering, or dirty soda — gets documented the same way: what the pitch was, what worked, what didn't, and what the result was.

Vendor management

Managing accounts, not just winning them.

A first sale is the easy part. This is what keeps an account healthy once Citreve is the incumbent supplier.

  • Reorder cadence tracking — knowing when an account is due before they have to ask
  • Quality checks and feedback loops on every shipment
  • Account health tracking — usage trends, expansion opportunities, at-risk signals
  • Direct outreach to Honey Dew Donuts franchisees — both locations already supplied and those not yet using Citreve — building relationships across the full franchise system, not just the accounts already won
The feedback loop

Share what's working. Share what isn't.

Every account — Honey Dew, Chicago, the Utah dirty soda chains — generates real information about what pitch, what content, and what outreach actually moves a buyer. That feedback doesn't stay anecdotal; it feeds directly back into the collateral and content, not just the next outreach attempt.

Replication

Chicago is the template, not the ceiling.

Chicago was chosen for speed and efficiency — an existing engagement already on the calendar, not a bet that it's the uniquely best market. Once the playbook is proven there, the same approach — authority content, named target list, LinkedIn social selling ahead of an in-person touchpoint — repeats in the next city or the next channel, using what months 1-3 actually taught rather than starting from a blank page each time.
